Explore Sandpaper Categories

Learn everything about sanding, polishing, and surface finishing — organized into professional categories for every project type.

Wood sanding

Practical sanding methods for solid wood: surface leveling, grain preparation, and grit selection that delivers clean cuts and predictable finishing results.

Furniture Refinishing

Step-by-step furniture restoration: safe finish removal, controlled sanding sequences, and surface prep that helps stains and topcoats lay smooth and even.

Trim & Molding Sanding

Clean up baseboards, casing, and profiles with controlled hand sanding and sponges, removing fibers and nibs so primer and paint bond smoothly and uniformly.

Drywall Sanding

Flatten joints, blend seams, and feather patches using mesh, poles, and dust control so walls and ceilings hit a paint-ready smoothness with minimal cleanup.

Floor & Deck Sanding

Run coarse-to-fine grit progressions to level boards, erase wear, and prep wood floors or decks for stain and finish with consistent color and durable adhesion.

Auto Body Sanding

Panel prep and feather-edging for filler, primer, and paint: manage scratches, straighten transitions, and build a uniform surface that finishes cleanly.

Wet Sanding Clear Coat

Level orange peel and refine clear coats with fine wet grits, then polish to clarity while controlling edges, burn-through risk, and final gloss uniformity.

Headlight Restoration

Restore lens clarity by wet sanding oxidized plastic through fine grits, then polishing to remove haze and improve nighttime visibility and overall appearance.

Metal Polishing & Rust Repair

Remove rust, blend scratches, and polish steel or aluminum with staged abrasives that prepare panels and parts for coating, plating, or a bright finish.
